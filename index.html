Facial Recognition

Your Faceprint is Everywhere

The facial recognition software that Orwell predicted in 1984 has been fully realized today. One of the leading companies using facial recognition is Clearview AI. The New York Times describes Clearview by saying, “Clearview is ... using billions of photos from the public internet. By testing legal and ethical limits around the collection and use of those images, it has become the front-runner in the field.”

Although the United States does not have any laws prohibiting facial recognition at a national level, some states like Illinois require companies to obtain consent from customers to use their “faceprint.”

The PATRIOT Act

With Security Comes Surveillance

A real-life government program that increased surveillance in the name of National Security was the PATRIOT Act. Following the attacks of September 11, Congress signed the PATRIOT act into law to prevent future terrorist attacks. To do so, they gave the FBI more access to private files and loosened restrictions on raids.

The American Civil Liberties Union insists that the act was unconstitutional because of its violation of search and seizure procedures. They point out that the bill allows the “FBI to force anyone at all - including doctors, libraries, bookstores, universities, and Internet service providers - to turn over records on their clients or customers.”

PRISM

The NSA's Internet Surveillance Program

The Enemy of the State film predicted government digital monitoring programs. One of these programs was called PRISM, which according to the New York Times allows the government to access “Online communications data” including “e-mail, chat services, videos, photos, stored data, file transfers, video conferencing and logins.”

Although the database is meant for international individuals, Americans who communicate abroad can be included. The tech companies involved include giants like Apple and Google, though the government insists it can only access the data through court orders. Edward Snowden leaked reports of the program in 2013.

Nerve Online Reality Game

In this universe, Nerve is an online game where "watchers" pay "players" high sums of money to complete the dares. The "players" must obey the following rules.

  1. All dares must be documented and recorded on the player's phone

  2. If players fail or don't complete the dare, they lose the money earned

  3. Players cannot report the game to the police

Although the game starts out with low-stakes dares, the plot thickens when the main characters are forced to take on bigger and bigger dares.
